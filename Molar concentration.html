What is Meant by Molar Concentration?

In chemistry, the molar concentration also called the molarity of the chemical solution is defined as the amount of substance per unit volume. The molar concentration can be easily calculated by dividing the molar number of moles of the substance by volume of the solution. In simpler terms, the molar concentration is the number of moles to solute dissolved in per litre of solution. The molar concentration formula is given by
Molar concentration (Molarity) = Amount of substance in moles/ volume of the solution.
