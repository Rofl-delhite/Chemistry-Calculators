What is Meant by Ionic Compound?

In chemistry, an ionic compound is defined as a chemical compound which is made up of ions which are held together by the electrostatic force known as ionic bonding. The chemical compound formed is neutral because it has both positively and negatively charged ions. The positively charged ions are called cations, and the negatively charged ions are called anions. For example sodium chloride, NaCl, which is nothing but Na+ and Cl–.
