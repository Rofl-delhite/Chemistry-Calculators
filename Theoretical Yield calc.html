What is Meant by Theoretical Yield?

In a chemical reaction, the theoretical yield is defined as the amount of product predicted by stoichiometry based on the number of complete reactants. Whereas the actual yield is defined as the amount of product actually produced by the reactant in the chemical reaction. The percentage yield can be calculated by dividing the actual yield to the theoretical yield. The theoretical yield is represented by moles or grams. The theoretical yield formula is given by
