What is Meant by Enthalpy?

In Physics, enthalpy is defined as the sum of the internal energy and the product of the pressure of the given thermodynamic system. It represents the state of the system. It is not a measure of heat transfer. In short, enthalpy is used to measure the energy of the system. Enthalpy is expressed by the letter “H”. Thus, the formula to calculate enthalpy is given by:
Enthalpy, H = U + PV
Where,
U – Internal Energy
P – Pressure
V – Volume
