What is Meant by Activation Energy?

In Chemistry, activation energy is defined as the least amount of energy which is required to stimulate the atoms or molecules to undergo the chemical reaction. Generally, all the chemical reactions require activation energy, so that the reactants can move together, and start breaking the bonds. The activation energy is represented by Ea. It does not depend on the pressure, temperature, volume, coefficient of the reactant, or the concentration of the solution.
